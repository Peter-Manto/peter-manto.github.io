About Me

I am a second year Echols Scholar and College Science Scholar at the University of Virginia studying Physics and Economics and a minor in Computer Science. My research has been in experimental nuclear and particle physics and I am currently interested in new computational physics research.

Charlottesville is where I grew up as well as where I attend school; if you see me downtown or biking up Emmet Street, I am always happy to stop and say hello.

If you would like to reach out to me, send me an email using the links on either end of this page and I will get back to you as soon as possible. My resume, GitHub, and LinkedIn are also at the bottom.

Research

I worked with the spin physics group led by Gordon Cates at the University of Virginia. Our group worked in a collaboration with Jefferson National Laboratory to probe the structure of the neutron through spin-exchange optical pumping (SEOP). I assisted with producing, preparing, and testing the helium-3 cells used as electron scattering targets at Jefferson Lab.
